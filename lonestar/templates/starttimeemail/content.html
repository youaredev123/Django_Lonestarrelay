We want you to have a great race and unforgettable weekend!  Here are all the details you need to know toward that end!
 
 
 
The TIP!

The Texas Independence Party is under the tent on Gonzales' downtown Confederate Square on Friday (April 1st) at 7:30PM!  DJ Johnny and Julie Loco will be playing the songs, and Lone Star Beer will generously provide hydration & carbs.  There will only be snacky-type foods, like M&Ms and popcorn. So, you'll definitely want to plan on having dinner elsewhere.  Please support one of the preeminent cities in Texas History (our wonderful host city of Gonzales!) by eating at one of their many fine dining establishments! 

The party will be a GREAT time!  Dancing with DJ Johnny and Julie (who have been with the TIR for 9 years now!), M&Ms, Popcorn, Lone Star beer... oh, and TIR tattoos... We will have plenty of TIR tattoos! But, to us, the best part about Friday night is hanging out with dear friends on Gonzales' historic downtown square.  The party will go until about 10pm or shortly thereafter, and each year a few teams are a tremendous help by assisting in quickly picking the place up... thank you so much!!


Check-In & Packet Pick-up

Packet pick-up will be at the Start Line in front of the Memorial Museum (414 Smith Street).   You may claim your race swag either on Friday night from 5 to 7pm, or on Saturday starting at 5:45am and extending until the last team takes off!   Please note: there is no packet pick-up at the party! 

At check-in, Team Captains, must show that the team has reflective gear and flashlights (and/or headlamps).  This year, everyone acknowledged the waiver when registering in order to register, so the ONLY teams that need to bring paper waivers are those with team members who are minors under 18 (in which case the waivers must be signed by the minors' parents).

Overnight Accommodations

The Gonzales Junior High (right next to the starting line) is able to accommodate any teams that wish to stay overnight on Friday.  Someone will be there to welcome you (and accept the $5 dollar suggested donation) from 9:00 – 11:00PM.  The door to go to is on the southeast side of the school (on St. Louis Street, right here).  After 11:00PM, the doors are locked.  People (we don't even know who they are) will kindly drag you and your sleeping bag out by around 8AM on Saturday.  On the one hand, it would seem like the 20 cannon blasts between 6-7AM would have woken up those staying in the Junior High.  On the other hand, we've seen some of you at the TIP...  Anyway, showers (without complimentary towels, sorry), an open floor, and a number of highly working electrical outlets are all available at the Junior High!


On Saturday night, most teams will choose the opportunity to stretch out and rest at Brazos High School (just 2 miles east of Exchange 21 on Hwy 36 in Wallis). This is the best choice for Saturday night! The wonderful folks there use the $5/person donation as a fundraiser for their annual Teacher Appreciation Banquet, so let’s help pick-up their TAB!  They offer the same sort of amenities (again, bring towels if you need them), and they also have coffee, too!


 


The Course and TIC-TOC!

FIRST, AND MOST IMPORTANTLY, IT IS UP TO YOU TO KNOW WHERE YOU ARE RUNNING.  The runners / teams that do not adequately review and know the course maps, directions, and descriptions beforehand tend to not fare as well (and that can be frustrating for everyone).  Here is a link to a PDF of all the Leg maps.

The TIR course is very similar to last year's, but there are several relatively minor changes. They are:
 - Exchange 22 is now after the left turn onto FM 1489 (Simonton Rd), making Leg 22 a little bit longer and Leg 23 a little shorter.
 - Coming into downtown Houston (Leg 32), runners will head straight on Allen (which becomes Lamar) and will stay on Lamar until they reach Smith.  At Smith, they will take a right and finish. 
 - Leg 33 will take Caroline (instead of La Branch) all the way down to Hermann Park, and Exchange 33 will be right at Sam Houston's Statue!  We have written up detailed directions for teams/drivers to avoid getting caught up in the Kite Fest and delayed in the park here.
 - Leg 34 will now start off by going around the West side of Hermann Park and Zoo (instead of fighting with the kite flyers (lol))!
 - Leg 38 is slightly shorter ending in front of the new Keller Junior High at Magnolia & Rose (and this will make Leg 39 a little longer).
 - Lastly, Leg 40 will be just slightly shorter, and the epilogue will take a more direct route to the San Jacinto Monument! 

We have always had to be ready for last minute changes to get around George Bush Park if it were flooded (it is a flood reservoir after all), and it is again looking like this could be the year.  So, by Wednesday of next week, we will make a call on whether or not we must take the alternate routes (which would change Legs 27 and 28 - making both a little shorter). Those maps will be available on the Relay Resources page of the site.
 
As always, while the course is marked with a ton of care and love, it is an open course and teams / runners themselves are always responsible for staying on course.  To rely on the course markings and / or another runner is an error in judgement.  

The types of markers you will see are:
1)    Cones (with white and black arrow stickers)
2)    Signs (on the dirt roads)
3)    Chalked arrows (red dot, blue dot, and white arrow)


 

The best Team Captains print out / cut out the leg directions, and give them to their team members to have during their legs.  Team Captains may also assist team members in advance through the mental math of, “If I run about 9:30 minutes per mile, then I will approximately reach the turn at 1.75 miles in….???”   Finally, the Leg Descriptions (see pdf below) should be required reading for anyone running the TIR (in combination with the Written Directions).  Finally, all drivers should familiarize themselves well with the Driver Directions.  All of these (and more!) can be found on the Relay Resources page of the site, and they are beyond helpful toward helping your TIR go as smoothly as possible! Good Luck!!

Who's Chasing People Down!

Talk is cheap, so we'll let the race determine this year's winners!  That said, the following are a few of the teams we're keeping an eye on...  
Hooked n' Horny - The last team to start usually means something, and we're planning on starting these guys from the University of Texas Running Club last.  This team has been with the TIR for 7 years now (and the club has 4 teams representing this year), and each year, the fast team keeps getting better and better.  Kyle Ray will be the captain for the second year, and we believe they have done some smart studying.

Austin Front Runners - Fast and Fabulous, The Planks (4.0 and 5.0), Corpus Christi Roadrunners, Sisters with Blisters, HCSS Fast Track, and Chevron Accelerators are all returning teams that are certain to compete well in their respective categories.
